Why Earthquakes Happen

Earth's outer shell is broken into massive plates that slowly drift on the mantle below. Where plates meet, they:

Collide (Himalayas forming), Separate (Mid-Atlantic Ridge), or Slide past (San Andreas Fault).

The stress builds until rocks suddenly slip â€” releasing energy as seismic waves we feel as earthquakes.

We cannot predict earthquakes. Despite decades of research, no reliable method exists to predict when and where the next earthquake will strike. We can only identify high-risk zones.

The Ring of Fire

Notice how earthquakes cluster around the Pacific Ocean? This "Ring of Fire" marks where the Pacific Plate meets other plates. About 90% of the world's earthquakes occur here.

Magnitude Scale

Each whole number increase represents 10x more ground motion and about 32x more energy.

A magnitude 6.0 releases as much energy as 15,000 tons of TNT. A 7.0 releases 32 times more than that.
